## Introduction

The **zebra** is a member of the horse family (*Equidae*) and is one of Africa's most recognizable mammals. Its distinctive **black-and-white striped coat** has fascinated scientists, artists, and nature enthusiasts for centuries.

Zebras are **herbivores** that spend much of their day grazing on grasses. Although they resemble horses, they have evolved unique adaptations that help them survive in the African savannas and grasslands.

---

## Species

There are **three living species** of zebra:

- **Plains Zebra (*Equus quagga*)**
  - Most common species
  - Found across eastern and southern Africa
- **Mountain Zebra (*Equus zebra*)**
  - Lives in rocky, mountainous regions
  - Excellent climber
- **Grévy's Zebra (*Equus grevyi*)**
  - Largest zebra species
  - Narrow stripes and large rounded ears
  - Endangered

## Physical Characteristics

Some notable characteristics include:

- Black-and-white striped coat
- Strong legs adapted for running
- Large ears for excellent hearing
- Short upright mane
- Average lifespan of **20–30 years**

### Typical Measurements

| Feature | Range |
|----------|-------|
| Height | 1.2–1.5 m |
| Length | 2.0–2.6 m |
| Weight | 175–450 kg |
| Top Speed | ~65 km/h |

## Habitat

Zebras inhabit a variety of environments, including:

- Grasslands
- Savannas
- Woodlands
- Semi-arid regions

### Habitat Preferences

1. Open spaces with good visibility.
2. Reliable water sources.
3. Abundant grazing areas.
4. Nearby shelter from extreme weather.

## Diet

Zebras are primarily **grazers**.

Their diet includes:

- Fresh grasses
- Dry grasses
- Leaves
- Bark (occasionally)
- Shrubs

### Daily Routine

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| Morning | Grazing |
| Midday | Resting |
| Afternoon | Grazing |
| Evening | Drinking water |
| Night | Remaining alert in groups |

---

## Social Behavior

Zebras are highly social animals.

A typical family group consists of:

- One stallion
- Several mares
- Their foals

Larger gatherings may form **herds** containing dozens or even hundreds of individuals during migration.

### Communication

They communicate using:

- Vocal calls
- Ear movements
- Facial expressions
- Tail movements
- Body posture

---

## Predators and Defense

Common predators include:

- Lions
- Hyenas
- Leopards
- African wild dogs

When threatened, zebras may:

- Run at high speed
- Stay close together in a herd
- Kick with powerful hind legs
- Bite attackers when cornered

> **Note:** A zebra's kick is powerful enough to seriously injure or even kill a predator.

## Conservation

The conservation status of zebras differs by species.

| Species | Conservation Status |
|----------|---------------------|
| Plains Zebra | Near Threatened |
| Mountain Zebra | Vulnerable |
| Grévy's Zebra | Endangered |

Major threats include:

- Habitat loss
- Competition with livestock
- Illegal hunting
- Climate change

Conservation efforts focus on:

1. Protecting natural habitats.
2. Supporting wildlife reserves.
3. Monitoring populations.
4. Reducing human-wildlife conflict.

## Conclusion

Zebras are remarkable animals whose distinctive appearance, social behavior, and adaptability make them an important part of African ecosystems. While some species remain relatively widespread, others face significant conservation challenges. Continued protection of their habitats and populations is essential to ensure these striking mammals remain a familiar sight in the wild for generations to come.
